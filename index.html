Why Aerate?

Over time, soil becomes compacted from foot traffic, mowing, and natural settling. When this happens, water, air, and nutrients struggle to reach the roots of your grass. Lawn aeration solves this problem.

Core aeration removes small plugs of soil from your lawn, allowing it to breathe again. This simple process delivers big benefits:

  • Stronger root growth by improving oxygen flow to the soil
  • Better water absorption, providing easy root access and reducing runoff
  • Improved fertilizer effectiveness so nutrients reach the roots
  • Thicker, healthier grass that crowds out weeds
  • Reduced soil compaction caused by heavy use and clay soils

Aeration is especially important in Colorado’s clay-heavy soils and dry climate. Most lawns benefit from aeration at least once per year, typically in the spring, to recover from winter and get ready for growth through the summer.

Why Fertilize?

Fertilizer provides essential nutrients that grass needs to grow thick, green, and healthy. Even well-watered lawns can struggle without proper nutrition. Fertilizing strengthens your lawn from the roots up and helps it recover faster from stress.

  • Promotes thicker growth by supplying key nutrients like nitrogen
  • Improves color for a greener, healthier-looking lawn
  • Strengthens roots for better drought and heat resistance
  • Helps prevent weeds by encouraging dense turf growth
  • Speeds recovery from Colorado's harsh weather conditions

Fertilizer is essentially a multivitamin for your grass. Applying it consistently throughout the year keeps your lawn strong and healthy. Spring is one of the most important times to fertilize, and doing it right after aeration allows nutrients to soak deeper into the soil where they’re needed most.
